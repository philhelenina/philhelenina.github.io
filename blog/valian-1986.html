Syntactic Categories in the Speech of Young Children

Valian (1986)

Study Details:

  • Age Group: 2;0, 2;5
  • Mean Length of Utterance (MLU): 2.93-4.14

Key Findings

Examined six syntactic categories:

  • Determiner
  • Adjective
  • Noun
  • Noun Phrase
  • Preposition
  • Prepositional Phrase

Main Observations

  1. Children showed evidence of all syntactic categories
  2. Children are sensitive to abstract, formal properties of speech
  3. Syntactic knowledge emerges earlier than previously thought

Theoretical Perspectives

Semantic Viewpoint: Semantic roles presuppose phrasal sentence segmentation

Syntactic Viewpoint: Grammatical relations defined over syntactic categories

Developmental Viewpoint: Category acquisition provides insights into syntactic knowledge acquisition

Methodology

The study synthesized different research methods to evaluate children's spontaneous language production and compare their performance against established linguistic category criteria.

The research drew from:

  • Brown's distributional analysis
  • Bloom's "rich interpretation"
  • Chomsky's work on taxonomic limits

Conclusion

The goal was to understand how young children develop and use syntactic categories in their early speech. The findings suggest that children comprehend complex linguistic structures much earlier than previously thought.
